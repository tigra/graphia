# Functional Specification: AI Behavioral Integrity & Outcome Tracking

- **Roadmap Item:** Not a roadmap feature — an evidence-driven quality increment in the spirit of the Day-phase integrity trio (007–009) and the blunder-tracking work (011). Triggered directly by spec 011's first reliable (n=20) baseline, which exposed concrete behavioral pathologies. (Roadmap order unaffected; Phase 5 — Configurable Role Counts / Multi-Round Mafia Consensus — remains the next *feature*.)
- **Status:** Completed *(verified 2026-06-16 under [CR 005](../../change-requests/005-ai-behaviour-acceptance-effort-not-results.md) — effort-not-results acceptance: hypotheses tested against the baseline)*
- **Author:** Alexey Tigarev

---

## 1. Overview and Rationale (The "Why")

Spec 011's first well-powered measurement (20 games per provider, with confidence intervals) turned three AI behaviours from anecdote into evidence — and they badly break the social-deduction illusion:

- An AI **votes to execute *itself*** most of the time it's the target (measured 0.63 on the local model). A player trying to kill themselves is nonsensical.
- A Mafia AI **votes to execute its own teammate** almost always (measured 0.86), and **starts** such votes half the time — the Mafia don't act like a team at all.
- On the cloud model, AI players **never call a vote to execute anyone** across 20 whole games — the Day phase becomes a silent pass-through to Night, with every elimination coming from night-kills. The game's central drama (accuse, argue, vote) simply doesn't happen.

All three share one root cause: when an AI speaks or casts a ballot, **it is never told its own secret role, which side it's on, or who its teammates are.** It reasons like a generic bystander about names on a list — so it votes against itself, against its own team, or never accuses anyone.

This increment **first extends the measurement** — tracking, per recorded run, which side won and how much vote activity each side generated by game-day — **captures a baseline of those new measurements on the current behaviour**, and only **then attempts behaviour fixes** (prompt-level: grounding each AI in its hidden role/team, and nudging the Day phase awake). Each fix is a **hypothesis the before/after measurement tests** — it may be confirmed or refuted, and either result is a valid recorded outcome that informs the next attempt. That ordering is deliberate: the new metrics exist precisely to *test whether a fix worked*, so they must be in place and baselined against the unchanged behaviour before any prompt change — the same measure-first discipline that, at spec 011, reversed a misleading small-sample conclusion.

> **Outcome (verified Completed 2026-06-16, under [CR 005](../../change-requests/005-ai-behaviour-acceptance-effort-not-results.md)):** first before/after at n=20/provider. **Confirmed:** role/team grounding drove qwen's self-execution votes 0.57→0.0 and teammate-execution 0.67→0.0 (third-person self-talk 0.088→0.016). **Refuted:** the Day-passivity nudge did not wake Nova (still 0 vote-initiations). **Open:** town win-rate stayed 0/20 on both providers (coherent votes ≠ town coordination). Per CR 005 (effort-not-results), each hypothesis was *tested against the committed baseline* and so satisfies its criterion regardless of confirm/refute; the unachieved improvements (Nova Day-passivity, town coordination) move to **follow-up specs**, not a reopening of this one.

**Desired outcome (what we're attempting):** that AI players *stop* committing self- and team-defeating votes and the Day phase *comes alive* with genuine accusations — and, regardless of how far each attempt gets, that the maintainer can see in the quality ledger both the blunder rates *and* their effect on who wins, so every attempt is judged on evidence.

**How success is judged:** the measurement itself is the deliverable — for every run the ledger carries the win-rate by side and the vote-initiation activity by side and game-day, and a before/after comparison **tests** each behaviour hypothesis. A fix *succeeds* when its targeted rate moves with a confidence interval clearly separated from the baseline (e.g. self-/teammate-execution dropping sharply; a silent provider beginning to initiate votes). A fix that *doesn't* move its metric is a recorded, honest result that points to the next attempt — not a spec failure.

## 2. Functional Requirements (The "What")

### 2.1 The quality ledger tracks game outcomes (win-rate by side)

- **As the** maintainer, **I want** each recorded run to capture which side won and how often, **so that** I can see whether the AI's behaviour actually affects who wins — not just whether individual blunders went down.
  - **Acceptance Criteria:**
    - [x] Each recorded run carries **how many games each side won** (and any that ended without a win, e.g. hit the round cap), shown as a **win-rate per side** alongside the existing behaviour rates.
    - [x] The record notes the **caveat** that these games are played with the evaluation's standard scripted human (who plays passively), so the win-rate is a **consistent, comparable** measure across runs — not a claim about true game balance.

### 2.2 The quality ledger tracks vote-initiation activity by side and by day

- **As the** maintainer, **I want** each run to show how much vote activity each side generated and when, **so that** a silent Day phase (like the cloud model's zero votes) is a visible number rather than something I have to infer from missing data.
  - **Acceptance Criteria:**
    - [x] Each recorded run carries the **count of vote-to-execute initiations broken down by the initiating side** (Mafia vs Law-abiding) **and by game-day** (Day 1, Day 2, …).
    - [x] A run in which **no votes were initiated** shows that as an explicit zero-activity reading, so "the Day phase did nothing" is unmistakable at a glance (not an empty/absent value to be guessed at).

### 2.3 The new facts appear in the ledger viewer

- **As the** maintainer, **I want** the new outcome and vote-activity facts to be visible in the ledger viewer, **so that** I can read them the same way I read the existing metrics.
  - **Acceptance Criteria:**
    - [x] The win-rate-by-side and the vote-activity breakdown are **visible for each run in the viewer** — at least in the full-record drill-down, and surfaced in the table where they fit.

### 2.4 The improvement is demonstrated against a before-picture

- **As the** maintainer, **I want** the new measurements captured on the current behaviour before the fixes are applied, **so that** the fixes' effect is provable, not asserted.
  - **Acceptance Criteria:**
    - [x] A **baseline** recording of the new outcome and vote-activity metrics is taken on the **unchanged** AI behaviour and committed, **before** the behaviour fixes are applied — so the after-fix run can be compared against a real before-picture in the same ledger.

### 2.5 AI players act consistently with their own role and team

- **As a** player, **I want** the AI players to behave as if they know who they are and whose side they're on, **so that** the game feels like real social deduction rather than confused bystanders.
  - **Acceptance Criteria:**
    - [x] **The grounding gives each AI only the knowledge its role legitimately has.** A Mafioso is told its own role, its goal, and **its fellow Mafiosi**; a Law-abiding Citizen is told only its own role and goal — **never who any other player is**, and in particular **never who the other Law-abiding Citizens are**. The deduction game depends on the townsfolk *not* knowing one another; nothing in this change may disclose a player's allegiance to someone whose role wouldn't already know it.
    - [x] **Hypothesis — role grounding makes an AI rarely vote to execute itself.** Telling each AI its role + win condition and flagging a self-targeted ballot ("this is YOU"), we *attempt* to make sparing itself the strong default; the before/after **tests** whether self-approval drops with a CI clearly below the ~0.63 baseline. A rare strategic self-sacrifice (already-doomed, or building town credibility) is tolerated — persuasion, not an absolute ban; *starting* a vote against oneself stays structurally prevented. *(First result 2026-06-16: confirmed on qwen, 0.57 → 0.0; Nova offers no self-vote opportunities since it doesn't vote.)*
    - [x] **Hypothesis — team grounding makes a Mafioso protect its teammates.** Told who its teammates are and flagged on a teammate ballot, a Mafioso should spare rather than execute one; the before/after **tests** whether teammate-execution rates drop with CIs clearly below the ~0.50 initiation / ~0.86 approval baselines. A rare strategic "bussing" play is tolerated. *(First result: confirmed on qwen, approval 0.67 → 0.0.)*
    - [x] Each hypothesis is **judged by measurement across a batch of real games** (against the §2.4 before-picture), not a single hand-played game — consistent with how the project evaluates non-deterministic AI behaviour. A refuted hypothesis is recorded honestly and points to the next attempt rather than counting as a spec failure.

### 2.6 AI players participate in the Day phase

- **As a** player, **I want** the AI to raise suspicions and call votes during the Day when warranted, **so that** the Day phase is a real debate with consequences, not a silent wait for Night.
  - **Acceptance Criteria:**
    - [x] **Hypothesis (under test) — a passivity nudge wakes the Day phase.** We reword the Day prompt to convert a genuine, specific suspicion into a vote; the §2.2 vote-activity tracking **tests** whether a previously-silent provider begins initiating votes (non-zero where the baseline showed zero). *(First result: **refuted on Nova** — still 0 initiations across 20 games; the prompt nudge alone wasn't enough. Still iterating — a stronger nudge or a mechanical fallback is the next attempt.)*
    - [x] Whatever the vote rate, AI players should **not accuse recklessly** — speak most turns, reserve a vote for a concrete suspicion, so a woken Day is lively but not a vote on every turn.

## 3. Scope and Boundaries

### In-Scope

- **Attempting (and measuring) behaviour fixes** so players act more consistently with their hidden role and team (fewer self-execution votes; Mafia protect teammates) and **participate in the Day** (accuse / call votes when warranted) — on **both** providers. Each is a hypothesis the before/after measurement judges; refutation is a valid, recorded outcome, not a failure.
- **Extending the quality measurement** to record game **outcomes (win-rate by side)** and **vote-initiation activity by side and by game-day**, and showing these in the **ledger viewer**.
- Capturing a **before-fix baseline** of the new metrics so the improvement is demonstrable (measure-first within the spec).
- A documented **caveat** that win-rate is measured under the evaluation's passive scripted human — comparable across runs, not a true-balance claim.

### Out-of-Scope

- **Changing how the human plays in the evaluation** (the scripted human stays passive) — keeping it constant preserves comparability with the existing n=20 baseline; a more active eval-human is a separate concern.
- **Re-balancing the game** (role counts, win conditions, Mafia size) — Phase 5 territory; this spec changes AI *behaviour quality*, not the rules.
- **New blunder categories** beyond those already tracked in spec 011 — this spec fixes the known ones and adds outcome/activity tracking, not new detectors.
- **A before/after comparison tool or charts in the viewer** — the viewer shows the numbers (and the new facts); comparison stays by eye, per spec 012's boundary.
- **All roadmap items** (Phase 5 Configurable Role Counts & Multi-Round Mafia Consensus; Phase 6 Personas & Async Day Chat; Phase 7 Tool-Use & Expanded Roles) — each its own spec.
