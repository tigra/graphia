# Graphia — Backlog

An **ungroomed** backlog — quick notes on ideas, gaps, and follow-ups that aren't yet
thought through enough for a spec or the roadmap, captured so they aren't lost between
sessions. Items here are raw: some will graduate to a spec (`/awos:spec`) or a
[`roadmap.md`](roadmap.md) feature, some will trigger a change request or an ADR, and
some will be dropped. Add items with the **`/backlog`** command.

Roadmap *features* live in [`roadmap.md`](roadmap.md); this file holds **follow-ups,
robustness gaps, measurement ideas, docs debt, and housekeeping**. It may be loosely
structured by section (as below) — keep it light.

## Follow-up specs — AI quality (evidence-driven, measured against the eval ledger)

- **Repetition reduction** — the top unsolved AI-quality problem. AI Day-speech repetition sits at **~0.36–0.45 (name-masked near-dup) on ollama** across the n=20 baseline, the post-013 run, *and* a live 4+1 game — untouched by the spec-013 grounding fix or the lineup. Spec 009's single anti-parrot prompt line is plainly insufficient. A dedicated fix (stronger anti-parrot prompt / context handling / temperature), A/B'd via `make repetition-experiment` + `make blunder-eval` against the recorded baseline, under the **effort-not-results** acceptance principle ([CR 005](../change-requests/005-ai-behaviour-acceptance-effort-not-results.md)). _Origin: spec 011 baseline + live play 2026-06-16._
- **Day-speech connectedness (measure + improve)** — AI Day utterances often have little or no connection to what other players just said or to the live topics; players talk past each other in parallel monologues rather than responding, agreeing, or rebutting. Measure a **connectedness** signal (does an utterance engage recent speech / the current topic? — how to measure is TBD) and improve it. Distinct from *Repetition reduction* (restating the same point) — this is about *engaging with others at all*; disconnected speech also starves deduction and town coordination. A/B-able under effort-not-results. _Origin: user request 2026-06-21._
- **Day-speech concreteness vs emptiness (measure + improve)** — AI players lean on empty general phrases ("stay vigilant", "we must be careful who we trust") instead of **concrete references to a specific player's actual statement or behaviour**. Measure how concrete vs empty utterances are across a game — this likely needs **human eval or an LLM-judge** rather than a cheap lexical metric (concreteness resists string-matching) — and improve it. Distinct from *Repetition* (same point restated) and *Day-speech connectedness* (engaging vs talking past): this is about *substance/specificity*. Empty filler gives the town no evidence to reason from — relates to *Own-goal prevention / accusation grounding*. A/B-able under effort-not-results. _Origin: user request 2026-06-21._
- **Nova Day-passivity** — the cloud model never initiates a Day vote (`vote_activity by_side {0,0}`), so its Day phase is silent and the town can't win. Spec 013's prompt nudge was **refuted** on Nova. Next attempt: a mechanical fallback (force a vote when the Day goes quiet, e.g. auto-nominate the most-discussed player) — a Day-*flow* change. _Origin: spec 013, refuted hypothesis._
- **Town-coordination / Day-decisiveness** — the town wins **0/20** on both providers even when individual votes are coherent (qwen post-013): coherent per-vote behaviour ≠ town coordination. Likely its own spec (richer Day strategy / decisiveness). _Origin: spec 013, open result._
- **Day-decisiveness levers — remaining (spec 018 n=10 review)** — the spec-018 day-round recap is accurate every round but **AI players never reason from it**; the town still wins **0/10** and **7/10 games stall to `no_winner`** (the model loops placid filler past the step budget without ever executing anyone). The feed-recap-into-prompts and per-AI-private-thoughts levers were **promoted to the Phase-6 roadmap** (2026-06-19); two levers remain here, each A/B-able under the effort-not-results principle: **(a)** **force-a-vote / Day-termination** as the Day nears the round cap (auto-nominate the most-discussed living player) — generalises the Nova force-vote idea above to qwen (reinforced by the 024+025 n=5 investigation: game-05 **deadlocked Day 1 then called zero votes Day 2** on the same two known suspects — a correct read dropped for want of Day-flow pressure); **(b)** check & strengthen **rule-awareness / situation-recognition** in the prompts — do players actually grasp that executing the *living* (not eulogising the dead) is how the town wins, and where the game currently stands? _Origin: spec 018 n=10 ollama review 2026-06-19._
- **Persona realism & stress-reactivity (spec 018 n=10 review)** — generated personas collapse into one calm, naive "stay focused on the facts" voice; distinct setups (handyman, librarian) yield no distinct speech, and nobody escalates as neighbours die. Fixes: **(a)** strengthen persona salience in the Day-speech prompt (it is out-competed by a generic placid attractor) and/or add a persona-distinctiveness metric; **(b)** generate a wider temperament range — include **vigilant / suspicious / assertive** archetypes, not only calm ones; **(c)** define **behaviour under stress** — a night kill or an execution is high-stakes, and personas should react with urgency rather than platitudes; **(d)** stop **persona-backstory bleed** into game reasoning (a Mafioso named his legend's wife "Sarah" as a suspect) — backstory characters are flavour, not players at the table. _Origin: spec 018 n=10 ollama review 2026-06-19._
- **Mafioso personas should lean dark-triad** — a Mafioso's hidden `true_self` should skew toward dark-triad traits (Machiavellian/manipulative, callous, self-serving) so its deception and its willingness to kill spring from a coherent inner motive — while its **public legend** stays free to pose as kind, warm, or upstanding as cover. Shifts only the *true-self* content of the existing two-layer Mafioso persona (spec 016, *AI Character Personas* — `true_self` vs public legend); the public face is unchanged. Plausibly also sharpens Day-phase agency (a darker inner self has clearer reasons to manipulate, steer, and accuse), bearing on the town-coordination / Day-decisiveness gap above. A persona-generation prompt change, A/B-able under the **effort-not-results** principle ([CR 005](../change-requests/005-ai-behaviour-acceptance-effort-not-results.md)). _Origin: user request 2026-06-20._
- **No cross-call persona-diversity guarantee** — each AI player's persona is an **independent** `get_large()` call (temp 0.7) sharing the same system + role template, differing only by the interpolated `{name}`; no call sees the others, so a mode-seeking model collapses the roster toward one modal voice — the structural root cause behind *Persona realism & stress-reactivity* above (which a per-name nudge and temperature don't fix). Options to actually guarantee spread: **(a)** generate the **whole roster in one call** and instruct the model to differentiate them; **(b)** **thread already-generated personas** into each later call ("make this distinct from these…"); **(c)** **pre-assign distinct archetypes/temperaments** (a fixed spread — vigilant / brash / dry / warm / anxious …) and have the model only flesh each out; **(d)** add a **distinctiveness check** (lexical/embedding similarity) with regen-on-collision — which also yields the persona-distinctiveness metric. A/B-able under effort-not-results. _Origin: persona-generation review 2026-06-21._
- **Own-goal prevention / accusation grounding** — in the 024+025 n=5 investigation, **3/5** games' only execution was **Law-abiding-on-Law-abiding** on content-free "vibes" tells (e.g. "you've been quick to accuse", "you emphasised thinking too much"), which the Mafia gladly ratified with Yes to launder a citizen kill through the town — the real Mafia were never even put to a vote. Spec 024's "don't get fellow Law-abiding executed" caution isn't holding. Require an **evidence-grounded reason** (cite a concrete prior statement / vote / night outcome) before a citizen calls or supports an execution; relates to the rule-awareness lever and persona realism. A/B-able under effort-not-results. _Origin: 024+025 n=5 transcript investigation 2026-06-21._

## Robustness gaps

- **Graceful career-greeting degradation** — a transient stats-store failure (e.g. expired SSO) currently **crashes the game at boot**: `render_greeting(store.load())` in the UI driver propagates `UnauthorizedSSOTokenError` and the game exits before starting. The greeting should degrade gracefully ("career stats unavailable") rather than take down startup. Same class as the spec-010/011 cloud-at-boot issue. _Origin: live smoke 2026-06-16._
- **AI self-name stutter in Day speech** — AI day-speech occasionally emits a leading self-name prefix, e.g. `Ivan: Ivan: I mean…` (3 such lines in the spec-018 n=10 run, which tripped the `third_person_self_talk` detector). Strip a leading `<own-name>:` prefix from AI speech output (defensive post-process at the speak site), or tighten the speak prompt/parse. _Origin: spec 018 n=10 ollama review 2026-06-19._

## Test reliability

- **Suite-wide ledger-write guard (belt-and-braces)** — a test-isolation bug let eval tests append ~25 synthetic records to the committed `evals/blunder-ledger.yaml`: `append_record`'s `ledger_path=LEDGER_PATH` was an **early-bound signature default**, so `run_eval`'s no-arg append always hit the real ledger and per-test `monkeypatch.setattr(LEDGER_PATH)` never reached it (**root cause fixed 2026-06-18** — the default is now `None`, resolved to the module global at call time). **Recommendation:** add an **autouse fixture pointing `blunder_eval.LEDGER_PATH` at `tmp_path`** for the whole suite, so no future eval test can touch the real ledger even if a redirect is forgotten; consider the same for `TRANSCRIPTS_ROOT`. _Origin: ledger pollution discovered 2026-06-18 during the spec-017 smoke._

## Measurement / eval ideas

- **Lexical → semantic repetition metric** — the `repetition` metric is lexical (difflib ≥0.85 on name-masked text); it may **undercount player-perceived repetition** when the same point is reworded (semantic, not near-verbatim). A future refinement (embedding similarity) if we want the metric to track perception. The 024+025 n=5 run sharpened this: aggregate lexical repetition fell to **0.29** (lowest recorded) yet all five transcript readers still saw heavy **verbatim** looping within a Day — concrete evidence the lexical metric can move while perceived repetition persists. Deliberate cheap-deterministic choice for now (spec 009/011). _Origin: live observation vs measured 0.36; reinforced by 024+025 n=5 2026-06-21._
- **Repetition-vs-lineup sweep** — spec 014 made the lineup a *recorded, controllable* eval variable (`settings.lineup` + `--citizens/--mafia`). A quick 4+1 read (~0.36) sits in the 5+2 band, so lineup doesn't obviously move repetition — but a proper sweep could confirm, and a repetition A/B should pin the lineup. _Origin: spec 014._
- **Engagement / decisiveness metrics (+ blunder-denominator caveat)** — the self/peer-vote blunder rates read **~0 only because the AI town barely votes** (spec-018 run denominators were 4 / 5 / **1**; Wilson CIs up to 0–79%), so a "clean" blunder rate can mask total non-engagement rather than signalling good play. Add engagement signals to the ledger/summary — **votes-initiated-per-game**, **% games resolved vs `no_winner`** — and surface them next to the blunder rates so the two are read together. _Origin: spec 018 n=10 ollama review 2026-06-19._
- **Active-human eval variant — the clear town-win unblocker (now evidence-backed).** The scripted human always votes No and never initiates. The n=5 024+025 transcript investigation (run `2026-06-20T21-15-30`) showed this passive baseline is the **binding constraint on town wins, not deduction**: in a 5+2 game the active town tops out at **3 Yes**, while the passive human's No + the two Mafia self-No's form a permanent **3-No block** — so a *correct* vote on a Mafioso caps at **3–3 and fails**, and the only votes that pass are own-goals the Mafia amplify. The town **correctly identified and repeatedly voted the real Mafia in 2/5 games yet could never convict.** An opt-in **active-human** variant (sometimes votes Yes / initiates) would break the 3–3 deadlock and let spec-024/025's real effect show — the **highest-leverage eval change; promote toward a spec.** _Origin: spec 018 n=10 review 2026-06-19; sharpened by the 024+025 n=5 transcript investigation 2026-06-21._
- **Titled, stats-labelled transcripts (render + viewer)** — make a preserved game identifiable at a glance instead of a bare "game 1". **(a)** Add a `<title>` element inside `<transcript>` (in `render_transcript`) naming the matchup by side — the Mafiosos vs the Law-abiding players, **by name** (e.g. `<title>Mafia: Bo, Dario — vs — Law-abiding: Mira, Avery, Sanaa, …</title>`), placed near the top alongside `<setup>`; the transcript already reveals true roles, so this is just a one-line roster summary. **(b)** In the `make view-ledger` transcript browser (`TranscriptListScreen`, spec 017), list each game by that **title plus per-game stats** (outcome/winner at minimum; rounds, votes, blunders if available) rather than `game-NN` — capturing per-game stats at transcript-write time if the run record doesn't already carry them. _Origin: user request 2026-06-20._
- **End-of-game recap / final standings in the transcript** — the transcript should carry a recap once a side wins, **including when the game ends on a Mafia night kill** (no closing Day), so an analyzer can easily read **how many Mafiosos and Law-abiding remain at the end and who they are** (final survivors by side, by name). Complements the `<endgame>` block (spec 022) and the titled-transcripts item above. _Origin: user request 2026-06-20._
- **Split-panel `view-ledger` (transcripts beside the YAML)** — show the ledger YAML and the transcript list on **one screen as side-by-side vertical panels** instead of separate screens: **left/right** switches the focused panel, and **up/down** within a panel either selects a transcript (transcript panel) or scrolls the YAML (YAML panel). A `make view-ledger` (spec 012) navigation/UX change. _Origin: user request 2026-06-20._
- **Search in `view-ledger` (`/` to find)** — add an incremental, vim/less-style search triggered by **`/`** in the `make view-ledger` viewer (spec 012) to jump to matches within the ledger view. _Origin: user request 2026-06-20._
- **Fold the round number into the `<round>` tag attribute** — render the per-Day round as `<round n="N">` instead of a bare `<round>` tag plus a separate `Round N.` content line, consistent with the `<vote initiator=… target=…>` / `<player name=… role=…>` attribute style. A transcript-render refinement (spec 021 round labels / spec 022 format). _Origin: user request 2026-06-20._
- **Vote activity by side — initiated-against vs voted-against** — break out day-vote metrics in evals along two distinct, often-divergent axes, each attributed by the **target's** side: **vote-initiation against a side** (initiating an execution vote on a Law-abiding vs on a Mafioso — who gets *nominated*) and **votes against a side** (Yes-ballots landing on a Law-abiding vs a Mafioso — who actually gets *executed*). Tracking **both** exposes where nomination and follow-through diverge; optionally also cut by the **actor's** side (LA- vs Mafia-driven). Sharper than aggregate vote counts — surfaces whether the town aims at Mafia rather than at its own — bearing on the town-coordination / Day-decisiveness gap. Extends the existing `vote_activity by_side` signal and the *Engagement / decisiveness metrics* item above. _Origin: user request 2026-06-20._
